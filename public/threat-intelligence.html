Threat Intelligence FAQ

What is cyber threat intelligence (CTI)?
Cyber threat intelligence (CTI) is the collection, analysis, and dissemination of information about current and potential cyber threats. It includes strategic intelligence (executive briefings, industry trends), tactical intelligence (IoC feeds, SIEM rules), and operational intelligence (threat actor TTPs, campaign tracking). CVEPulse delivers all three levels through consulting services and a free real-time intelligence dashboard.
What are Indicators of Compromise (IoCs)?
Indicators of Compromise (IoCs) are forensic artifacts that identify potentially malicious activity. Common IoC types include IP addresses, domain names, file hashes (MD5, SHA-256), email addresses, and URLs associated with threat actors or malware. CVEPulse provides real-time IoC feeds and an enrichment tool that returns threat context, MITRE ATT&CK mapping, and risk scoring for any indicator.
How does CVEPulse track trending and critical CVEs?
CVEPulse aggregates CVE data from CISA KEV (Known Exploited Vulnerabilities), NVD, and ransomware intelligence feeds to identify trending, critical, and emergency CVEs in real-time. Our threat posture scoring system weighs active exploitation evidence, ransomware campaign correlation, and CVSS severity to surface the vulnerabilities that actually matter.
What SIEM platforms does CVEPulse integrate with?
CVEPulse integrates with all major SIEM platforms including Microsoft Sentinel, Splunk, IBM QRadar, LogRhythm, and Elastic Security. We provide custom detection rules, IoC feed ingestion, webhook-based alerting, and threat intelligence platform (TIP) implementation services.
Is the threat intelligence dashboard really free?
Yes. The CVEPulse Community dashboard provides free access to real-time threat posture scoring, CISA KEV feeds, ransomware tracking, emerging threat alerts, and 3 IoC lookups per day. Professional ($49/mo) adds unlimited lookups, PDF reports, campaign intelligence, and API access. Enterprise plans include custom feeds and SIEM integration.
